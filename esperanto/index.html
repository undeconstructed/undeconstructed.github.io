Esperanto

Why

I consider esperanto to be the obvious choice as an auxillary language, and I hope the need for such doesn't need arguing. esperanto is not a perfect language, but it combines logical construction with naturalness in a way that makes it very usable. also it is able to be understood by people who don't have a perfect knowledge of it.

Changes I use

Standard Esperanto is very male-default. I have no right to suggest the language be changed, but I choose to use it with a neutral default:

  • All existing "male" are gender neutral, including "li"
  • The "-iĉ-" suffix is the male version of "-in-"
  • The "hi" pronoun is copied from English

As a plus, this makes viro neutral, and so it can replace plenkreskulo, which is far too long a word. a summary of related words:

  • hom/o - a human
  • infan/o - a human child
  • person/o - an individual of a thinking species
  • vir/o - an adult person (of any thinking species)
  • knab/o - a non-adult person
  • in/o - a female
  • iĉ/o - a male

Miaj patroj estas kaj du homoj, kaj du viroj. Ili estas mia patrino, kiu estas virino, kaj mia patriĉo, kiu est viriĉo.

"Virhomiĉo" estas homo kiu estas kaj vira kaj iĉa. eble "viro" estas same, sed kiam oni ne bezonas precizi la sekson aŬ la specion, kial fari?

Also I use the word sinistra to replace maldekstra. there is no hierarchy between left and right, and so it seems unfair that left-handedness be considered an opposite.
