A humane manifesto

Aimed at the UK. Written in one morning, with no regard for economics. 2025-08-11.

Recognise that:

  • people are both independent and interdependent
  • we can't live without the natural world
  • humanity is what makes our lives important
  • trust is based on transparency

Aim to:

  • Create a stable, sustainable, rational society, in which people work for societal and personal fulfilment.

Short term focus:

  • democratisation
    • fair representation: alternative vote or similar
    • two elected houses: 1) by region, to represent people, 2) by organisation, to represent interests
    • honesty in all campaigning
    • no outside income for representatives - any income creates conflicts of interest
    • separation of state from monarchy and from church
  • transparency of commerce
    • brands must be transparent - who owns, who manufactures, who takes responsibility
    • adverts may not say or imply anything that they cannot offer
  • simplification of finance
    • no over-complicated business structures
    • no HFT, no complex derivatives, etc
    • companies should not act outside their defined purpose
  • less waste
    • no importation of mass-produced, low quality items
    • reduction of disposable items of all kinds
  • community projects
    • supplies, locations, organisational tools, etc made available for volunteer organisations
    • using land to support people, not cars
  • enforcement of responsibilities
    • failure to follow road rules -> no right to drive
    • corruption -> no right to hold authority

Longer term goals:

  • more honesty, more rationality, less manipulation, no corruption
    • any person or organisation with power must be open about how it operates
    • anti-rational organisations to be marginalised
  • education with a focus on evidence-based decision making
    • everyone taught to consider, and to reject dogma
    • life-long learning to the norm, with education available at all ages
  • simplification of regulation, with more community involvement
    • if people are willing to take part in discussion and democracy, then regulation can be lighter
    • those who attempt to avoid the spirit of regulations must be punished
  • shared ownership of essential utilities: health, water, power, communication, transport
    • an essential service should not seek to extract profit
    • non-essential commerce will continue as ever, within restrictions on advertising etc as mentioned
  • reduce interaction with non-aligned societies
    • no arms exports to theocrats or dictatorships; eventually no trade with them at all
    • support interaction with individuals in such places
  • encourage and support interaction between individuals and between societies, e.g. through common languages
    • alienation of individuals or of groups must be avoided everywhere
    • people should be able to communicate fully and freely, without need for machine mediation
  • reduce consumption
    • people should have a path to fulfilment that does not depend on wasting resources
    • encourage people to recognise that the drive to have new things is caused by manipulation
  • advancements to be shared
    • aiming to reduce obligatory work through automation and elimination
    • if an advance can fundamentally change society for the better, then it must be done for the benefit of everyone
  • wealth shall be limited if necessary
    • there should not be a need to acquire huge wealth when there is less to consume
    • but if people try anyway, they should be limited
  • no obligatory labour, except for a fair share of essential work
    • work should be done for the purpose of achieving personal or shared goals
    • if a person is forced to do an unfulfilling job for lack of options, society as a whole is not fulfilled
    • society needs people to do essential work, and to support social goals - those unwilling or unsuited may choose to support in other ways
